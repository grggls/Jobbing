## Job Description
VP of Engineering, Architecture and Transformation
Remote, US
GitLab is the intelligent orchestration platform for DevSecOps. More than 50 million registered users and more than 50% of the Fortune 100 trust GitLab to ship better, more secure software faster.
Role Summary:
As the VP of Engineering, Architecture and Transformation, you'll create and lead a new Engineering Leadership Team (ELT)-level engineering pillar focused on AI-powered productivity, next-generation architecture and horizontal execution across GitLab.
You'll tackle critical cross-cutting problems such as improving engineering productivity with AI tools like Claude Code, solving major architectural challenges like decoupling the monolith and building next-generation primitives, and improving responsiveness by embedding a horizontal engineering team into high-priority verticals.
Two new charters:
1. Architecture, Quality and Engineering Excellence
2. Horizontal Engineering
What you'll do:
- Lead the new Engineering, Architecture and Transformation pillar as part of the ELT
- Drive definition, adoption and continual improvement of engineering best practices
- Own the 'Customer Zero' charter — championing internal use of GitLab, unblocking teams, directly contributing to code when needed
- Define and guide the transition to next-generation, AI-optimized architecture (monolith decoupling, core primitives)
- Build and lead Architecture, Quality and Engineering Excellence team to deliver non-linear productivity gains through AI-assisted development (Claude Code, GitLab Duo)
- Establish Horizontal Engineering team as a flexible, embedded group
- Champion operationally frugal mindset (COGS optimization, high availability, 99.99% reliability)
What you'll bring:
- Large-Scale Engineering Leadership: Proven experience leading large-scale engineering organizations, including horizontal or platform functions spanning multiple product areas
- World-Scale Service Operations: Deep background in building, operating and evolving highly available, world-scale services
- Architectural Expertise: Experience driving major architectural transitions (monolith decoupling, foundational primitives)
- Hands-On Technical Leadership: Ability to engage directly in code and technical design
- AI-Assisted Development: Expertise in AI-assisted development and tooling, track record using AI to improve developer productivity
- Cost-Conscious Architecture: Experience driving COGS-conscious engineering decisions
- Cross-Functional Influence: Ability to influence without direct authority in a matrixed environment
- Product-Minded Change Leadership: Managing the human side of large technical shifts
About the team:
ELT-led engineering pillar drives platform-wide impact across GitLab. Small, high-context, globally distributed group that works asynchronously. Responsive coverage during US business hours through embedded engineers.
Example projects:
- The AI-Augmented Delivery Engine: Using GitLab as 'Customer Zero' by integrating Claude Code or equivalent to automate non-creative tasks, establishing a blueprint for non-linear productivity gains.
- The 'Act 2' Alignment Charter: Strategic roadmap identifying systemic bottlenecks and defining architectural evolution from DevSecOps platform to AI-autonomous delivery engine.
