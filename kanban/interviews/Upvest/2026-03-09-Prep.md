Pillar 3: FinOps and Observability Cost Management
Situation: Mobimeo's cloud costs exceeded €300K/month with no visibility or ownership. Then New Relic quoted close to €1M for the next year's contract — while our usage was spiking.
What I did:
- Audited AWS usage across 6 accounts, identifying underutilized and orphaned resources.
- Ran a hack week project with the cloud platform team to implement Grafana — delivered cost and utilization dashboards plus ownership tagging in a week.
- Migrated service dashboards, performance monitoring, and SLOs off New Relic and into an open-source observability stack (Prometheus, Grafana, Loki, Jaeger) within two months. Eliminated the need to renew the New Relic contract.
- Enforced guardrails via IaC, right-sized instances, and reserved capacity. Brought engineering into the budgeting process with product-level FinOps reviews.Outcome: Reduced cloud spend by 25% per quarter for three consecutive quarters. Created a culture of cost-aware engineering and SLO ownership. Passed multiple Deutsche Bahn audits with clean infrastructure documentation.Tailoring note: The New Relic migration is a strong story for any company on expensive commercial observability tooling. The broader point: FinOps isn't a finance exercise — it's an engineering culture change. Teams need to see their own costs, understand the trade-offs, and have tooling that makes cost-awareness a byproduct of good engineering, not extra work.
